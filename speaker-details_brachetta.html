Title

Optimal Self-Protection via BSDEs for Risk Models with Jump Clusters

Author

Matteo Brachetta

Abstract

In this talk we explore some recent advances in optimal control problems in Insurance Theory, with a special focus on self-protection strategies. This framework describes an insurance contract where the policyholder can choose both the level of protection, i.e. the covered losses, and the self-protection effort, which are the preventative actions taken to reduce the frequency of the claim arrivals. These strategies are assessed based on the utility maximization of the terminal wealth, incorporating a final reimbursement that can incentivize the buyer to increase her effort. We show that this problem can be formulated in terms of a suitable BSDE, for which we prove existence and uniqueness of the solution. Then, the optimal strategies are evaluated in terms of the BSDE solution.
